# DreamPlay Homepage Merchandising Map

## Goal
Turn the newly simplified homepage into a real DreamPlay storefront homepage that feels intentional, premium, and product-led — without trying to solve the full shop/catalog problem yet.

## What this homepage should do
The homepage should not try to show every product equally.

Its job is to:
- establish DreamPlay as a premium piano brand
- show one clear flagship product direction
- prove emotional fit for families and learners
- show that DreamPlay is more than a furniture object because it includes a learning/app ecosystem
- introduce bundle/accessory buying behavior
- hand users off cleanly into `/shop` and `/build-your-bundle`

## Recommended homepage positioning
Primary story:
- ergonomic premium piano design that helps reduce pain and strain for real players

Secondary story:
- DreamPlay is built for families, learners, and aspirational home use without giving up musical seriousness

Tertiary story:
- DreamPlay is not just a piano object; it also includes a learning/app ecosystem and complete-setup buying path

## Hero strategy
Recommendation:
- lead with one flagship hero product, not a whole product family

Why:
- the homepage needs one strong anchor
- multiple competing hero products will make the page feel like a catalog too early
- we can introduce range breadth lower on the page and on `/shop`

## Core product/message principle
The homepage should explicitly foreground the ergonomic keyboard story.

What should be clear very early on:
- DreamPlay is designed to reduce pain and strain
- the ergonomic sizing story is not generic comfort language; it is tied to actual keyboard sizing
- the two key size callouts Lionel wants emphasized are:
  - DS 5.5
  - DS 6.0

Practical implication for the homepage:
- hero and early product copy should not just say "premium" or "modern"
- they should clearly connect DreamPlay to smaller ergonomic key sizing and easier long-session play
- DS 5.5 and DS 6.0 should appear as named proof points in product/detail sections, even if the hero headline itself stays cleaner

## Recommended homepage section order

### 1. Hero
Purpose:
- establish brand, flagship product, and first CTA

Use:
- `Piano-Front-2.jpg` or `dreamplay-one-main-product-in-studio.avif`

Message job:
- DreamPlay is the ergonomic modern home piano designed to reduce pain and strain

Primary CTA:
- Shop Pianos

Secondary CTA:
- Build Your Bundle

What this section should emphasize:
- one flagship product image
- one clear headline
- one short subhead
- two clean CTAs max

### 2. Brand intro / positioning
Purpose:
- explain what DreamPlay is in one quick read

Message job:
- bridge from visual appeal to brand meaning
- define the product category in a more emotional/editorial way

Recommended content angle:
- beautifully designed pianos for modern homes, musical growth, and family life

### 3. Family / lifestyle proof section
Purpose:
- show the product in human use, not just isolated photography

Use:
- `dreamplay-hero-2-4.jpg`

Message job:
- emotional reassurance
- home fit
- learner/family relevance

CTA option:

Purpose:
- give the homepage one deeper merchandising moment on the core model

- ergonomic sizing designed to reduce pain and strain

- designed for everyday practice in the modern home

### 5. App / connectivity section
Purpose:
- show DreamPlay’s ecosystem advantage

Use:

Message job:
- learning support

- modern digital value

CTA option:

### 6. Bundle / complete setup section
Purpose:
- introduce higher-intent shopping behavior

Use:
- `Piano-Bench-Frontal-Bundle.png`

Message job:
- customers do not need to piece everything together alone

### 7. Premium / editorial halo section
Purpose:
- elevate brand perception with one luxury moment

Use:
- `Gold-DS-6.0-full.png`

Message job:
- DreamPlay is also a design object and premium statement

Important note:
- keep this concise so it does not hijack the homepage narrative

### 8. Testimonial / trust proof
Purpose:
- show social proof and confidence reinforcement

Message job:
- confidence before lower-page CTAs
- should support conversion rather than feel like filler

### 9. Founder / authority proof or value-props close
Purpose:
- close with legitimacy and practical reassurance

Use:
- `lionel-yu-carnegie-hall.png` if we want authority/editorial framing
- otherwise use the existing value-props section as the final practical close

Recommendation:
- keep value props as the final section on the first pass
- founder proof can appear just above it or be held for pass two if the page feels too long

## CTA map
Homepage CTA behavior should be simple.

Primary CTA destinations:
- `/shop`
- `/build-your-bundle`

Secondary CTA destinations:
- `/features`
- `/our-story`

Recommended distribution:
- hero -> `/shop`
- bundle section -> `/build-your-bundle`
- app/connectivity section -> `/features`
- one softer trust/editorial CTA -> `/our-story`

## What not to do on this first homepage pass
- do not try to feature every SKU equally
- do not turn the homepage back into a long theme demo
- do not lead with too many category cards
- do not over-index on comparison logic before the shop page is cleaned up
- do not make the gold halo product the main homepage identity
